# Death Scenario

## The Death (Summary)
**Method:** Falls 40 stories after miscalculating a rooftop gap jump between the hostel and adjacent building
**Category:** Self-Inflicted
**Duration:** 4.2 seconds (the fall) + instant (impact)
**Witnesses:** 6 hostel guests on rooftop, one filming

## Mechanism (Technical)

### The Cause
Massive blunt force trauma from 40-story fall onto concrete. Multiple system failure. Essentially instantaneous death on impact.

### The Setup
- Hostel in Cartagena with illegal rooftop access (staff looks the other way)
- Adjacent building approximately 8 feet away (Bryce estimated 5 feet)
- Night conditions reducing depth perception
- Alcohol consumption (3 beers) affecting judgment
- Peer pressure from fellow guests
- Crumbling edge on the hostel side, not visible in darkness

### The Trigger
Bryce begins his run-up to cheers of "Do it! Do it!" while a fellow guest films on his phone.

### The Process

1. Bryce takes his run-up position 15 feet from the edge
2. He begins sprinting, adrenaline masking the alcohol impairment
3. His plant foot hits the crumbling edge, which gives way 6 inches
4. Instead of launching from solid ground, he launches from collapsing masonry
5. The miscalculated distance + reduced jump power = 2 feet short of the opposite roof
6. Bryce's fingers graze the opposite ledge but cannot grip
7. 4.2 seconds of freefall - enough time to understand, not enough to process
8. Impact with concrete alley below

## Research Notes
- Terminal velocity for a human body is reached after approximately 12 seconds/1,500 feet of freefall
- A 40-story fall (roughly 400 feet) lasts approximately 4.2 seconds
- Impact speed would be approximately 110 mph
- Death is instantaneous from massive trauma
- Studies show the brain remains conscious for 1-3 seconds into a fall before "tunneling" occurs

## The Death Sequence (Full Detail)

### Setup (Chapter 35-37)
Bryce has been building toward this all week. He's practiced on lower rooftops. He's scoped the jump (from too far away, at daytime). He's mentioned it to everyone who will listen. The Australian group's mockery sounds like encouragement to him.

### Trigger (Chapter 38)
The rooftop party is peaking. Someone hands Bryce a third beer. Someone else says "Bet you won't." His GoPro is charged. Multiple phones are out. He can already see the video title: "INSANE CARTAGENA GAP JUMP - NEARLY DIED."

### The Death (Chapters 39-40)
Write this sequence in full detail:

**Second 0:**
Bryce rolls his shoulders. The crowd quiets. He takes three deep breaths, just like the tutorials taught him. He visualizes success. He thinks about the thumbnail for the video. He starts to run.

**Second 1-2:**
His feet pound the rooftop. The cheers start. Everything feels perfect, powerful, inevitable. His plant foot hits the edge and the world drops two inches. His brain screams ABORT but his body is already committed. The launch is weak, angled slightly downward.

**Second 3-4:**
He's in the air. The opposite rooftop isn't coming closer fast enough. He knows. His arms pinwheel uselessly. His fingers stretch toward the opposite edge. They touch. Cold concrete. For a fraction of a second, friction. Then sliding. Gone.

**Final Moment:**
The world rotates. The stars are below him now. Wait, no. The ground is coming up. The ground is very close. Bryce has one crystal clear thought: "Oh, so THIS is what dying fee--"

### Immediate Aftermath
Screaming from the rooftop. The filming stops. Someone vomits. The body is not recognizable as a body from above - just a shape and spreading darkness. The hostel staff arrives in panic. Police are called. The Australians delete their footage (except one who keeps it and will be haunted forever). The GoPro is recovered from Bryce's chest mount - it kept filming the entire fall and the moment of impact. It becomes evidence, then internet legend.

## Sensory Details

### Sight
- The last thing Bryce sees clearly: his own fingers against concrete
- The fall: streaked lights, spinning cityscape, the moon in the wrong place
- The witnesses see: a shape shrinking, arms spinning, then darkness

### Sound
- The cheers turning to screams
- Wind roaring past his ears during the fall
- A sound he doesn't hear: the impact (too fast for his brain to process)
- What the witnesses hear: a wet, crunching thud that no one forgets

### Touch/Pain
- The crumbling masonry under his foot
- Cold concrete brushing past his fingertips
- The wind tearing at his tank top
- He feels no pain from the impact - nervous system destroyed before signals can transmit

### Smell
- Rooftop: cigarette smoke, spilled beer, salt air from the Caribbean
- The fall: nothing, too fast
- Aftermath: copper and something worse

### Taste
- Last thing he tasted: cheap Colombian beer
- Final moment: adrenaline metal on his tongue

## The Horror Element
The horror is the 4.2 seconds. Long enough to understand what's happening. Long enough to regret. Not long enough to bargain, to accept, to do anything but experience pure terror. The GoPro footage that surfaces online shows those 4.2 seconds from Bryce's perspective, and it ruins everyone who watches it.

## The Comedy Element
The dark humor is in the perfectly composed final Instagram post still in his drafts: "Cartagena rooftops are calling. Tonight we fly." The irony of 847 followers becoming 84,700 after his death. The fact that he finally went viral, just not how he planned.

## Writing Notes

### Pacing the Death
- Slow, detailed build-up showing every ignored warning
- The death itself should feel fast but eternal - stretch the 4.2 seconds across pages
- Aftermath should be cold and clinical

### POV During Death
- Stay in Bryce's POV until impact, then switch to rooftop witnesses
- Let reader experience those 4.2 seconds in real-time
- Do not describe the impact from Bryce's POV - cut to black

### Graphic Content Level
- Scale: 7/10
- Show the fall in visceral detail
- Imply rather than describe the impact directly
- Show the aftermath through others' reactions, not close description
